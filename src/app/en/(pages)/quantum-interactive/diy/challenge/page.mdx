## QOOOL Kit DIY Challenge

Are you skilled with electronic components, comfortable with programming, creative, and able to design experiments? Do you enjoy sharing your discoveries with others? The only requirement is that you have a senseBox:edu. Are you interested in diving into quantum technology, especially quantum sensing, and sharing your experiment with others? Then apply for the QOOOL Kit DIY Challenge.

### Who can participate?

Eligible participants include school classes, extracurricular learning groups, educational institutions, universities, teachers, private hobbyists, and retirees from Germany. Companies and research groups are not eligible.

### How long does the challenge run?
You have time from April 1st to May 30th, 2026 to set up your experiment and submit your documentation to us.

### How does the challenge work?
If you are selected as a participant, you will receive one of ten QOOOL Kit DIYs from us free of charge.

![QOOOL Kit DIY](https://cloud.reedu.de/s/MwNKKfWnpfg6Dk6/download)

It includes an LED with microdiamond and microwave resonator, as well as a photodiode with color filter. You will build a simple experiment on a breadboard. Allowed are experiments that use either quenching (fluorescence detection without microwave excitation) or ODMR (fluorescence spectroscopy with microwave excitation). You will design an experiment around the QOOOL Kit DIY to detect magnetic fields and changes in magnetic fields, and you may use anything you have at hand, such as a 3D printer, hot glue gun, wood, your own measuring equipment, etc.

You will document your setup and experiment step by step, with photos and images, so that anyone can understand and replicate it. You will then provide this documentation to us free of charge. We will verify your setup and experiment and then make it freely available to everyone on our project website [qoool-sensing.org](https://qoool-sensing.org) as Open Source, crediting you as the original author.

Are you interested? Then apply now for the QOOOL Kit DIY Challenge by email. Let us know what equipment and experience you have with electronics and programming, and whether you already have a rough idea for your experiment.
